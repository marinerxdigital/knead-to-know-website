# Knead To Know Brand Guidelines

## Brand Overview

**Knead To Know** is a boutique home bakery located on **Daniel Island, South Carolina**. The brand should feel premium, coastal, local, handcrafted, clean, bright, trustworthy, and professional.

The visual identity should communicate artisan bread, cookies, homemade quality, warmth, and elevated Lowcountry charm without looking rustic, cluttered, cartoonish, or generic.

## Color Palette

| Color                   |       Hex | Use                                                     |
| ----------------------- | --------: | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Bright White            | `#FFFFFF` | Main website background, card backgrounds, form fields  |
| Lowcountry Coastal Blue | `#4F7EA8` | Primary CTA fill, borders, accent lines, badges         |
| Soft Harbor Blue        | `#7FA7C7` | Secondary accents, subtle borders, softer UI lines      |
| Deep Black / Soft Black | `#111111` | Primary text, headlines, strong contrast details        |
| Optional Deep Navy      | `#1F3447` | Alternative dark text/accent when black feels too harsh |

## Typography Direction

- Use elegant serif typography for premium headings, badges, and decorative CTAs.
- Use clean, readable sans-serif typography for body copy, forms, captions, product descriptions, and navigation.
- Maintain strong hierarchy: large clean headings, short descriptive body copy, and clear CTAs.
- Avoid overly playful, cartoon, handwritten, or generic bakery fonts.

## Logo Usage Notes

- Use the circular logo where a full brand mark is needed: header, footer, packaging, labels, social profiles, and invoice branding.
- Maintain clear whitespace around the logo.
- Do not stretch, skew, recolor, or crop the logo in a way that cuts off the badge border or Daniel Island text.
- On the website, pair the circular logo with a simple navigation system so the header remains clean and premium.

## UI Style Rules

- Keep the website bright white and clean.
- Use Lowcountry Coastal Blue sparingly for CTAs, borders, icons, and accent lines.
- Use black or deep navy for primary text.
- Use generous spacing and whitespace.
- Keep cards, forms, buttons, and icons minimal and refined.
- Use subtle shadows only when they improve separation without making the design heavy.
- Keep product presentation premium: bright photography, simple descriptions, clean pricing, and direct CTAs.

## Negative Design Rules

Avoid:

- Cartoon bakery styling
- Generic cupcake graphics
- Loud colors, gradients, clutter, or heavy textures
- Overly rustic farm-style visuals
- Low-contrast text
- Distorted logos or stretched UI assets
- Unrelated Spilled Milk branding, imagery, metadata, alt text, or product references
